Lorin Hollander’s career as a concert pianist is also shared with his performances as a renowned symphonic and choral conductor.

In addition to his work with symphony orchestras worldwide, Hollander is committed to conducting youth, college and university orchestras and choruses in rehearsal/explorations and performances of sacred music. These events explore:

  • Nurturing the beauty and humanity in music
  • Transforming the notes into an artistic breakthrough of creative joy
  • Mastering a musical instrument for a lifetime of self fulfillment
  • Empowering full self-expression and creative contribution through conducting as a new model for leadership


Lorin Hollander has specialized in conducting chamber ensembles and one concert possibility is to program two Bach clavier concerti conducted from the keyboard with a performance of Vivaldi’s Gloria for chorus and chamber orchestra on the second half. This is a cost effective performance if you require both a guest conductor and major soloist, here combined into one. The Bach/Vivaldi concert is a wonderful vehicle for both professional and university or youth orchestras and is an exciting enhancement to educational and community residencies. Whether a gala, baroque, standard symphony orchestra performance or a fundraising event, these concerts are an opportunity for the entire community to celebrate the glory of making music.
